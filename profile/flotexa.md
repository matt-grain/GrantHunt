## What FloteXa Does

FloteXa designs and deploys **vegetated floating islands** (ilots flottants vegetalises) to restore polluted lakes and water bodies. The core innovation combines **phytoremediation** (native plant-based filtration) with **mycoremediation** (mycelium/fungal networks) to naturally filter excess nutrients (nitrogen, phosphorus), capture heavy metals, reduce cyanobacteria blooms, and restore aquatic biodiversity.

The product -- **FloteX** -- is positioned as the **first 100% Quebec-made floating island**, using entirely local materials and native plant species. The company offers a turnkey service: site analysis, design, permitting, installation, monitoring, and end-of-life treatment (harvested biomass is valorized or safely disposed, creating a circular economy loop).

**Target customers:** Quebec municipalities and lake associations dealing with eutrophication. With 200+ eutrophied lakes in Quebec and regulatory pressure from the RAMHHS "no net loss" framework, there is structural demand for restoration services.

## Key Differentiators for Grant Applications

1. **Only Quebec solution combining phytoremediation + mycoremediation** -- mycelium integration is genuinely novel and differentiates from all competitors (FII, Biomatrix, etc.)
2. **100% Quebec supply chain** -- native plant species, local materials, local manufacturing. Strong alignment with Quebec economic development goals.
3. **INRS research partnership** -- academic credibility + access to Mitacs/NSERC collaborative funding streams
4. **Structural market demand** -- RAMHHS "no net loss" regulation creates legal obligation for wetland restoration, not just voluntary spending
5. **Circular economy angle** -- biomass harvested from mature islands is valorized (metal extraction, biomass reuse), not wasted. Unique among competitors.
